Our Book Collection

Adventure

Into the Wild

By Jon Krakauer: The true story of a young man, Christopher McCandless, who abandoned his comfortable life to live in the Alaskan wilderness. His journey is one of self-discovery, idealism, and a quest to escape from the constraints of society. Krakauer delves deep into McCandless's thoughts and motivations, unraveling a compelling tale of adventure, survival, and tragedy. This captivating narrative explores the risks and rewards of pursuing personal freedom in the wild, offering insights into the human spirit's complex relationship with nature.

Life of Pi

By Yann Martel: A thrilling tale of survival and spiritual exploration as Pi Patel, a young Indian boy, is stranded on a lifeboat in the Pacific Ocean with a Bengal tiger named Richard Parker. After a shipwreck, Pi finds himself alone in the vast ocean, forced to confront both the elements and his own beliefs. As he navigates the challenges of survival, he learns to coexist with the powerful and dangerous tiger, forming an unexpected bond. Through his journey, Pi grapples with questions of faith, the nature of reality, and the resilience of the human spirit. This powerful and philosophical novel blends adventure with deep reflections on survival, belief, and the meaning of life.

The Hobbit

By J.R.R. Tolkien: Follow Bilbo Baggins, an unassuming hobbit from the peaceful Shire, as he embarks on an unexpected adventure to reclaim a stolen treasure guarded by the fearsome dragon Smaug. Bilbo joins a group of dwarves, led by Thorin Oakenshield, on their quest to take back their homeland and fortune. Along the way, he encounters trolls, elves, goblins, and giant spiders, while uncovering his own bravery and resourcefulness. As Bilbo faces challenges that test his wits and courage, he discovers the true meaning of heroism and friendship. This timeless tale of adventure, magic, and self-discovery has captivated readers for generations, weaving themes of courage, loyalty, and the triumph of good over evil.

Non-fiction

Sapiens

By Yuval Noah Harari: *Sapiens* explores the history of humanity, from the emergence of Homo sapiens in Africa to our dominance today. Harari examines key events like the Cognitive and Agricultural Revolutions, the rise of shared myths and trade, and the impact of the Industrial Revolution on society and technology. He challenges readers to question our assumptions about progress, society, and the future, offering sharp insights into how we have shaped the planet and what it means to be human.

Educated

By Tara Westover: *Educated* is a powerful memoir that tells the story of Tara Westover’s journey from growing up in a strict and isolated family in rural Idaho to earning a PhD from the University of Cambridge. Despite the challenges of growing up in a family that rejected formal education and the outside world, Tara’s determination to learn and escape the confines of her upbringing propelled her through years of hardship. The memoir explores themes of resilience, self-discovery, and the transformative power of education, illustrating how one’s thirst for knowledge can break the cycles of oppression and lead to profound personal change.

The Body

By Bill Bryson: *The Body* is a captivating and humorous exploration of the human body, offering readers a fascinating look at the intricate systems that keep us alive and functioning. Bryson combines scientific facts with his trademark wit and curiosity to take us on a journey through the body’s various organs, cells, and processes, shedding light on everything from the brain's complex workings to the mysteries of our immune system. With his engaging storytelling, Bryson not only explains the science behind the body’s functions but also highlights the surprising and often bizarre aspects of human biology. Packed with interesting facts and historical anecdotes, *The Body* is a delightful, thought-provoking, and accessible read for anyone curious about the wonders of the human body.

Mystery

The Girl with the Dragon Tattoo

By Stieg Larsson: *The Girl with the Dragon Tattoo* is a thrilling mystery that delves into the dark undercurrents of a powerful family’s history. The story follows investigative journalist Mikael Blomkvist and the brilliant, but troubled hacker Lisbeth Salander as they work together to unravel the decades-old mystery of a missing young woman from one of Sweden’s wealthiest families. As they dig deeper into the family’s secrets, they uncover shocking truths that threaten to expose corruption, betrayal, and dangerous alliances. With its rich character development, complex plot twists, and a sharp critique of social and economic inequalities, Larsson's novel is a gripping exploration of revenge, justice, and the hidden darkness in people’s lives.

Big Little Lies

By Liane Moriarty: *Big Little Lies* is a captivating tale that explores the complexities of friendships, families, and the hidden lives of suburban women. The story revolves around three women—Madeline, Celeste, and Jane—who seem to have it all, yet are grappling with their own personal struggles. As their lives become intertwined through their children's school, long-held secrets and buried emotions start to surface. At the heart of the narrative is a mysterious tragedy that unfolds at a school trivia night, leading to a shocking crime. Moriarty masterfully unravels layers of deception, with keen observations on the pressure of societal expectations, domestic violence, and the devastating consequences of keeping secrets. With a mix of humor, tension, and drama, this novel explores the darker side of the seemingly perfect suburban life.

The Silent Patient

By Alex Michaelides: *The Silent Patient* follows Alicia Berenson, a famous painter who is found standing next to her husband's dead body, with no explanation and a refusal to speak. Criminal psychologist Theo Faber becomes obsessed with her case, uncovering dark secrets and unraveling a trail of obsession, betrayal, and shocking twists. This gripping psychological thriller will keep readers hooked until the final revelation.

Romance

Pride and Prejudice

By Jane Austen: *Pride and Prejudice* tells the story of Elizabeth Bennet, an intelligent young woman, and her evolving relationship with the wealthy Mr. Darcy. Set in 19th-century England, the novel explores themes of love, social class, and personal growth, as both Elizabeth and Darcy overcome their prejudices and discover deeper truths about themselves and each other. With wit and charm, Austen's classic continues to captivate readers with its timeless message of love and understanding.

Me Before You

By Jojo Moyes: *Me Before You* follows Louisa Clark, a quirky caregiver, and Will Traynor, a wealthy man paralyzed after an accident. Their unlikely friendship blossoms into a profound bond, challenging both to confront difficult choices about love, sacrifice, and life’s meaning. This emotional story explores personal transformation and the impact one person can have on another’s life.

The Notebook

By Nicholas Sparks: *The Notebook* is a heartfelt love story about Noah and Allie, who fall in love during a summer despite their differences. As time passes, their love endures, even as Allie faces Alzheimer's disease in later years. Noah, devoted to her, reads their love story from a notebook in hopes of rekindling their memories. This emotional tale explores themes of love, loss, and devotion, showing that true love transcends time and challenges.

Fantasy

Harry Potter and the Sorcerer's Stone

By J.K. Rowling: *Harry Potter and the Sorcerer's Stone* follows a young boy named Harry Potter, who discovers on his eleventh birthday that he is a wizard. As he embarks on his journey to Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry, Harry uncovers a world of magic, mystery, and danger. Along with new friends Ron and Hermione, Harry faces challenges and learns about his connection to a dark past, setting the stage for an extraordinary adventure that will change his life forever.

A Game of Thrones

By George R.R. Martin: *A Game of Thrones* weaves a tale of power struggles, betrayal, and intrigue across the fantastical continents of Westeros and Essos. With noble houses vying for control of the Iron Throne, alliances are formed and broken, and deadly secrets are uncovered. Amidst the political chaos, characters must navigate dangerous loyalties and unpredictable forces, leading to an unforgettable battle for power and survival.

The Name of the Wind

By Patrick Rothfuss: *The Name of the Wind* is the epic tale of Kvothe, a gifted young man with extraordinary talents in music, magic, and storytelling. As he recounts his journey from a troubled childhood to becoming a legendary figure, Kvothe unveils the mysteries of his past, the hardships he faced, and the powerful forces that shaped his destiny. This captivating fantasy explores themes of loss, love, and the pursuit of knowledge, making Kvothe's story one of triumph, tragedy, and self-discovery.
